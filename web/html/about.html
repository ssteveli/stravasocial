FAQ

Who created this project?

Segment Compare is an open source project created by Scott Stevelinck

Why?

As I entered the race scene, I wanted a way to compare myself against other athletes registered in the same race without the clicky clicky work of navigating Strava.

Any other reasons?

I wanted to learn a few new technologies for a new job I'm starting soon, so keep your comments to yourself about the code. ;)

Can I find you on Strava?

Yep.

I'm being asked for an athlete id to compare to, how do I find that?

Visit the profile page of an athlete you'd like to compare against, in your browser you'll see the athlete's id as part of the URL.

How does the comparison work?

Due to the way the Strava API works the comparison is based on the following workflow:

  • Gather your activites in the past ## (specified at runtime) of days
  • For each activity gather segments ridden
  • See if the athlete you're comparing against has ridden the same segment
  • If so, gather your top time and their top time for the segment
  • Add the segment to the results of the comparison

Will other types of comparisons (i.e. watts/kg) be added?

I'm limited to the functionality of the Strava API. As they expose more data, I'll add more comparisons.

What does the Weighted Average Difference (WAD) really mean?

The distance WAD is weighted according to the following:

  • Less than or equal to {{804.672 | feet:measurement_preference}} = 1 point
  • Less than or equal to {{1609.34 | feet:measurement_preference}} = 2 points
  • Less than or equal to {{2414.02 | feet:measurement_preference}} = 3 points
  • Less than or equal to {{3218.69 | feet:measurement_preference}} = 4 points
  • Greater than {{3218.69 | feet:measurement_preference}} = 5 points

The climbing WAD is weighted based on the segments average grade * 2

The time difference in percentages is tallyed out and weighted against the above points. This is the weighted average.

The bottom line: I'm limited by Strava's rate limiting. This means I can invoke their API so much across yourself and all the other individuals trying to run comparisons. I plan on working with Strava in the future to see if I can get the rates raised for the application.
